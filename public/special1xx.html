Experience the adventure of a lifetime on a tour to Machu Picchu. Your first steps will be on the busy, cobbled streets of Cusco, former Inca imperial capital turned Spanish colonial town, one of the oldest continuously inhabited cities in the Americas. Continuing to the Sacred Valley, you’ll visit small villages where the indigenous culture thrives and you’ll scale to the top of ancient ruins to reach stunning viewpoints.

You’ll board a train to get to Machu Picchu. Through the train’s panoramic windows, a collection of spectacular sights is on display: giant snow-capped peaks, rushing river rapids, and the deep green foliage of the Andean cloud forest. From the base town below Peru’s most majestic ruins, you’ll ride to the top of a mountain ridge, walk along an ancient path, and the Inca stone citadel will open up before you and take your breath away.

Day 1: Arrival to Lima

  • Upon arrival to the Lima airport, a Peru For Less representative will meet you and show you to your airport hotel.
  • Overnight in Lima.

View Gallery

Day 2: Arrival to Cusco

  • Make your way to the airport for your morning flight to Cusco. Your airport hotel is walking distance from the departure terminal.
  • Upon arrival to the Cusco airport, a Peru For Less representative will meet you for the drive to the historic center. On the way to your hotel, you will see examples of Spanish colonial architecture constructed upon the remains of ancient Inca palaces and temples.
  • Cusco is located at 3,400 meters (11,150 feet) of altitude. Be sure to give your body a chance to acclimatize with plenty of rest, light meals, and water. Cusco has many options for casual and fine dining.
  • Overnight in Cusco.

View Gallery

Day 3: Cusco City Tour

  • Spend a free morning in Cusco at your leisure. Explore the stalls of San Pedro Market, browse the art workshops of bohemian San Blas, or visit one of the many museums. All attractions are within walking distance.
  • In the afternoon, an expert guide will take you to many of the amazing archeological sites in and around the city, including the Cusco Cathedral in the Plaza de Armas and the Temple of the Sun or Qoricancha, as well as the ruins of Sacsayhuaman, Q'enko, Puka Pukara, and Tambomachay.
  • Spend the evening at your leisure. Check out Cusco Travel Guide for suggestions of what to see, what to do, and where to eat.
  • Overnight in Cusco.

View Gallery

Day 4: Sacred Valley Tour

  • Meet your expert guide for a tour of the Sacred Valley of the Incas. In Pisac, you’ll visit the famous market where locals gather to sell and barter fruits, vegetables, herbs and natural dyes. Shop the stalls for Andean textiles, handicrafts, and silver jewelry.
  • After a buffet lunch in Urubamba (not included), the tour continues to the impressive fortress ruins of Ollantaytambo. Originally the royal estate of the emperor Inca Pachacuti, the site later served as a stronghold for Manco Inca Yupanqui in the final resistance efforts against the Spanish. The tour will end at your Sacred Valley hotel.
  • Overnight in the Sacred Valley.

View Gallery

Day 5: Machu Picchu Tour

  • After breakfast, you’ll board the train to Machu Picchu. The final leg of your journey to the Inca citadel takes you from the Andean highlands into the cloud forest, past snow-capped peaks, sheer granite cliffs, dense vegetation, and the boulder-strewn Vilcanota River.
  • Upon arrival to the train station in Aguas Calientes, the town at the base of Machu Picchu, you’ll meet our Peru for Less representative for a transfer to the bus station and a 20-minute drive to the secluded citadel.
  • At the entrance gates, meet your expert Machu Picchu guide for a 2-hour tour. Later, you’ll have time to explore the ruins on your own and soak in this amazing site at your own pace.
  • In the late afternoon, you’ll return to the train station for the ride back to Cusco. Overnight in Cusco.
  • If you would like to spend more time at Machu Picchu, you can customize this package to include an overnight stay in Aguas Calientes and a second-day visit to the ruins.

Tipon, Piquillajta & Andahuaylillas Tour

Experience the "Southern Valley" of Cusco with an expert guided tour of Tipon, Piquillajta and Andahuaylillas. At Tipon, you will discover a remarkable and extensive Inca site famous for beautiful gardens irrigated with fresh, cool water that runs through an intricate system of fountains.

Piquillajta, “the City of Fleas,” is an impressive and ancient Wari ruin built in the first century. Here you’ll explore this administrative capital of the southern portion of the Wari Empire which dates back to pre-Inca times. You will also visit the small village of Andahuaylillas, home to a dazzling 17th-century church, known as the “Andean Sistine Chapel.” Built upon the site of an old Inca temple, the church’s altar is gilded in 24-carat gold leaf and adorned with both Christian and Quechua symbols.

Please note that lunch is not included in this tour. Meal options can be limitied in this area of Cusco, so we recommend bringing snacks or a boxed lunch, if you would like to eat during the tour. The tour will end in the early afternoon at your Cusco hotel. The tour will last approximately 4 hours.

Included: Transfer to/from Cusco, expert English-speaking guide and entrace tickets to Tipon, Piquillajta and Andahuaylillas sites.

Not included: Bottled water, food, tips.

*Note: Prices are per person and based on private tour with a minimum of two travelers. For single traveler pricing, please consult with a Travel Advisor.

Sacred Valley Horseback Riding to Maras and Moray

Enjoy a horseback riding adventure across the Sacred Valley to the Maras Salt Mines and the Moray agricultural terraces. The tour starts with a pickup from your Cusco hotel and a transfer to the Sacred Valley, about 2 hours from Cusco by car. Here you will start your exploration on horseback.

You will cross a bridge over the Urubamba River and head uphill out of the Sacred Valley. During the uphill portion you will ascend 1,300 feet out of valley and into the mountain plains. This portion will take approximately three hours with stops along the way to enjoy the views.

Next you will arrive at Moray, home to circular agricultural terraces and believed to be a horticultural experimentation center. Each terrace level represents a different altitude and climate zone, which allowed the Incas to develop crops that could be grown in a variety of locations. You will explore Moray with your guide before continuing on to Maras.

In the village of Maras you will stop for a picnic lunch before riding to the Maras salineras (salt ponds). Here salty spring water flows into polygonal pools, dries in the sun, and is then collected and packaged. You will tour this site with your guide to learn more about the history and how it is used today. After this visit a vehicle will meet you for your transfer back to Cusco by car.

The experience will last between 7 and 8 hours, with approximately 4 hours of riding time.

Included: Transfers to/from Cusco, English-speaking guide, picnic lunch and entrance tickets to Maras and Moray sites.

Not included: Bottled water, extra snacks, tips.

*Note: Prices are per person and based on a private tour with two travelers. For single traveler options, please consult with a Travel Advisor.
